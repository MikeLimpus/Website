The Life & Legacy of David Bowie

CIS 101 Creative HTML Page

David Bowie (real name David Jones), was a British musician and actor. He was one of the most prolific musicians of the 20th century, releasing 27 studio albums, along with many live albums, EPs, singles, and film soundtracks. He released his first album, called "David Bowie", in 1967. The next year, he released "Space Oddity", which was his breakout single. After this, Bowie released many influental singles, such as "Changes", "The Man That Sold The World", "Starman", "Diamond Dogs", "Heroes", and many more. He also starred in films such as Labyrinth, The Man Who Fell To Earth, and The Prestige. Shortly before his passing, he recorded and released his "swan song" album "Blackstar", which handles themes of mortality and death. On Janurary 10, 2016, two days after the album released, Bowie passed away from liver cancer. He was survived by his wife and two children.
